The weird stuff I learned

SODaLG 2026.04.01 조회수 1

Allo, name's SODaLG. In this post, I will attempt to sum up the weird, and somewhat bizzare things I remember from doing this assignment

To start, you can fit multiple classes into one element(?). No idea why. Then there are the many different types of displays. ranging from 'box' that stretches to each side, 'inline-box' that just surrounds the content, and 'flex' which can do quite a lot of things, including somwhat dynamic spacing. That is how I managed to get the footer to stick to the bottom, in fact. It squished the footer attribute or something all the way down there. You can also make it stack the elements or contents within a flex box using flex-direction. Now, there are a lot more to this single tool, but I will refrain from writing more as this is already giving me a migraine. Also, the vh tag apparently means the view window. Don't ask, I'm not proud of it. There is also the overflow tag/attribute used in css that essentially can determine how you handle excess text. There is also 'transition' that allows you to control state changes, this is how I did the animated buttons. And I know I promised, but flex-shrink basically stops a div or something of the sort from being compressed or squished away. Also, the hover pseudo-class taught me that there are actual ways that this nightmare of a language perform actions.

That is about all, and OH! I do recommend W3. Freaking amazing source.
